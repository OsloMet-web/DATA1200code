The semantic web and semantics in HTML

The entire debate about the Semantic web is far too long to be taken in a 5 ECTS course! But it is important if you choose to work as a web-/ front-end developer. Below are those elements that are most relevant for DATA1200, not necessarily those that will be most important for you.

Wikipedia actually has a good-ish introduction to the debate. But more relevant, Mozilla has a list of semantic terms.And w3schools has a good introduction to how semantic elements improves accessibility. (Go beyond the first page, please.)

Most relevant for you:

  • <header>, the top, often repeated between pages, often contains the <nav>
  • <nav>, in the top, often inside the <header>, usually the navigation menu
  • <main>, all the actual content
  • <footer>, at the bottom, often repeated between pages, often contains the contact-info
  • <article>, content, should have a heading, may be multiple per page, inside <main>
  • <section>, read the Mozilla guide before you use!
  • Please note that <div> is not a semantic element!

Semantic elements and reader-mode

If you want to trigger the reader mode in browsers, you should use at least the <main>. Below are two images of Firefox, the left-hand one in normal mode, the right-hand one in reader-mode. As you can see, the grey "footer" that is outside the <main>-element is not displayed in reader-mode.

The images, by the way, are in a grid. In reader-mode, rather than scale, the grid-elements are stacked below eachother.
